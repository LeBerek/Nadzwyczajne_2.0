Lack of Interoperability

European Vehicles running on their own wheels when they do not have the required interoperability in country forming part of the route or approval/authotisation to place in service become exceptional consignments. For such vehicles each application submitted for exceptional consignment should contain a note on missing vehicle's authorisation in concerned countries.

Missing authorisation

applies equally to vehicles which are empty, loaded or which themselves are the subject of a contract of carriage and which do not have the inscriptions or respective marks:

  • passenger coaches
  • RIC
  • freight wagons,
  • TEN GE or TEN G1
  • RIV
  • country code in authorisation
  • traction units and multiple units

Additional

Additional technical and operational information is required for transport of vehicles on their own wheels when vehicle authorisation is missing. Additional information required for application to IM can be obtained from the vehicle documentation or at least party from vehicle markings. Once elaborated it must be integrated into the approval application by the consigning RU or added as an Appendix C - page 37.

NOTE For vehicles towed on their own wheels without own power less additional declarations are to be declared in addition to the application. For vehicles which run under their own power IMs require more detailed declarations on compliance with the planned route.
