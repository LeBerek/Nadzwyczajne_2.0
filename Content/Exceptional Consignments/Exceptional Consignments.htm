Exceptional Consignments

Introduction

Rail Exceptional Transports represent the rail industry in a nutshell. Under certain circumstances, a loaded wagon or any type of a rolling stock may become an Exceptional Consignment (EC). In order to deal competently with customers ordering exceptional consignments and efficiently declare transports parameters to the infrastructure managers, one needs to be familiar with the numerous aspects of rail transport.

Your Knowledge

As a standalone professional or a member of exceptional transport team you are expected to hold broad knowledge and thorough understanding of rail.

You need to understand:

  • the restrictions imposed by rail infrastructure
  • rolling stock types and their key parameters importance for exceptional transports
  • the wide range of goods and the transport conditions they must meet while moved by rail
  • operational difficulties which exceptional consignments may cause

The User Guide

Railway Undertaking (RU) intending to move an exceptional consignment, applies to the Infrastructure Manager (IM) for a transport authorisation. The format of the transport authorisation request uses the UIC template with 38 codes which are adopted by the vast majority of RUs and IMs.

This guide is meant to be your handy source of information required for competent authorisation requests handling. It is meant to be mainly available online via computer, notepad or a mobile phone. Vast majority of information is provided through online (HTML5) output. In addition, for specific use-cases in the application see also a pdf. file, which you will find in the Handy chapter.

The User Guide's Navigation

The user guide is split into two main parts:

  1. Quick frontal access to an overview of the exceptional transport authorisations requests and the three groups of data. There you will find the specific application codes with examples of standard declarations.
  2. Upper /side menu access to the chapters. These chapters provide you with the normative background context of UIC exceptional consignments. The normative background is supported with examples of dedicated declarations for specific use-case examples.
