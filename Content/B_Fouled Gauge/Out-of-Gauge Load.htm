Out-of-Gauge Load

When wagon with goods exceeds the predefined dimensions of height and width of the loading gauge or a vehicle on own wheels exceeds the predefined vehicle's profile, then railway undertaking sdeal with the gauge foul. A wagon with load or a vehicle is considered as exceeding the gauge if:

  1. its contour due to its width or height, when positioned on a straight and level track, does not fall within the applicable gauge contour at least on the one section of the transport route under consideration,
  2. on the straight and level track contour falls within the gauge, but after application of the loading width restrictions from UIC Loading Guidelines, Volume 1 and Tables 21, 23 and 25 have not been complied with.

In such a case the transport of such a wagon and cargo set or a vehicle on own wheels might be still possible on the carriage conditions for an exceptional transport, on the route indicated and proved by an infrastructure manager.

Railway undertaking:

  • determines the smallest loading gauge on the entire route from the departure station to the destination station,
  • identifies critical points of the consignment,
  • suggest the preferred route
  • chooses approval method for the consignment
  • files an application form to the infrastructure manager for exceptional consignment.
  • or alternatively runs the consignment with combined transport conditions or with outline procedure.

Infrastructure manager approves the conditions with restrictions to run the consignmet and sends them to the railway undertaking.

In the extreme cases infrastructure manager might issue a refusal for the transport of the oversized consignment. In such a case the reason for issuing a refusal must be clearly declared by the infrastructure manager.
