Handover to Ferry

Most goods shipped by rail to ports are routed to harbour loading quays or port terminals, depending on their type and transfer method to the ship. In Europe, some trains are run to ports in rail-ferry communication. In the both cases of exceptional consignment trains to a port, it is necessary to:

  • ascertain to which specific tracks in the port area the train with the exceptional consignment is to be directed,
  • inquire whether exceptional consignment approval is also granted by the port infrastructure managers for this type of consignment.

Definition

Consignments which are to be transshipped to a ferry, when they do not meet the regulations of General Contract of Use of wagons (GCU), Appendix 14, Part A constitute the other type of exceptional consignment.

Use Cases

Rail-ferry transfer locations and specific rules are listed in Part A of GCU Annex 14. Each port and ferry has individual characteristic of access for a train and therefore some restrictions apply which wagon must fulfil to be accepted on a ferry. Observe wagon marking:

In these cases of wagons transfer to ferries the restrictions include also necessity for obtaining an exceptional consignment authorisation:

  • wagons with two- or three-axle bogies that do not meet the GCU Appendix 14 Part A
  • wagons with bogies that have more than three axles
  • shipments carried on more than one wagon
  • goods carried on two wagons coupled together
  • loads carried with a buffer wagon
