Vehicle Gauge

Vehicle gauge is one of the technical characteristics of a railway vehicle and concerns the permitted size of a vehicle. More precisely the vehicle gauge is a set of rules encompassing a reference profile and its associated calculation rules which determine the vehicle’s maximum exterior dimensions and the required clearance area within the infrastructure to prevent the contact of the vehicle with the structure gauge.

NOTE Rolling stock constructors apply one of the three calculation methods. Vehicle's conformity with a reference profile is declared with calculation method referred as static, kinematic or dynamic one. The calculation methods within a single reference profile are independent. That is, depending on the calculation method used, the vehicle design will make more or less use of the railway lines room capacity provided by the infrastructure manager. The static method is the oldest calculation method and the most conservative. At the same time, it is the simplest one to apply for evaluation of consignments dimensions in exceptional transports.

The infrastructure manager shall make available to market participants, in open access, information on the gauges that are compatible with their network. Railway undertakings may find information on the permitted vehicle gauges in the RINF (in gauging section), network statements, or IMs' instructions, for example.

In such case the only way to examine a vehicle is inspection of detailed vehicle's drawing or study restriction analisys calcultion. The later type of document is rarely made available to RUs.
